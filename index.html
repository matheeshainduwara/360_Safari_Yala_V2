Discover Yala

Sri Lanka's Wildlife Paradise

One of Asia's most spectacular national parks

World-Class Wildlife

Yala National Park, located in southern Sri Lanka, is renowned for having one of the highest leopard densities in the world. Home to elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and over 200 bird species, it offers an unparalleled wildlife experience.

Diverse Ecosystems

The park features stunning landscapes ranging from dense forests to open grasslands, wetlands to rocky outcrops. Each ecosystem supports unique flora and fauna, creating incredible opportunities for photography and wildlife observation.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Beyond its natural wonders, Yala boasts fascinating archaeological sites including the ancient Sithulpawwa rock temple. Explore centuries of history while immersing yourself in the pristine wilderness that surrounds these cultural treasures.

Unforgettable Adventures

Whether you're a wildlife enthusiast, birdwatcher, or nature photographer, Yala offers something extraordinary. Our expert guides ensure you experience the very best this magnificent park has to offer throughout the year.

Quick Facts

  • Location: 260km SE of Colombo
  • Entry Points: Katagamuwa, Palatupana
  • Temperature: 26.4°C (79.5°F)
  • Climate: Dry most of year
  • Best Visit: June - September
  • Open Hours: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM
